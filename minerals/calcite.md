Calcite is one of the most common minerals on Earth, forming the primary component of limestone and marble. It crystallizes in the trigonal system and displays an extraordinary range of crystal habits — from scalenohedra ("dogtooth spar") to rhombohedra and prismatic forms.

Calcite exhibits strong double refraction (birefringence): when placed on text, two images appear through the crystal. It effervesces vigorously in dilute hydrochloric acid, which is a standard field identification test for carbonates.
